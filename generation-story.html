Building the Sound of a Generation

The journey from raw freestyle to the finished sound that represents an era. This is how the music became a movement.

The first session was more than a recording—it was a gathering of a generation. Toxic invited young poets, DJs, and beatmakers from different corners of the city into one room. The goal wasn’t just to make a song; it was to capture a moment when the future of Nairobi’s sound was being written in real time.

Every chord, every verse, and every scratch was shaped by lived experience. The producers layered vintage vinyl textures with hard-hitting drums, while the writers pushed past clichés and landed on something honest. The track became a map of the streets and a reflection of the voices that refused to be ignored.

As the song evolved, so did the vision. What began as an experiment turned into a mission: to build a sound that felt both rooted in the culture and ready to break boundaries. Toxic kept the process open, bringing in collaborators and testing ideas until the energy felt right.

The result: a sound that speaks to a generation that is bold, restless, and unapologetically itself.
